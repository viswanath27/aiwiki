MEAN

Mean is the sum of all the samples and divided by its count. This really important metric to get a picture of the samples. Mean for population is represented by μ but mean for samples is represented by x̄

MEDIAN

Median is the middle number of the dataset whene it is ordered in ascending order.

MODE

Mode is the most repeated number in the complete dataset. This can give a representation of a value for the complete dataset

COVARIANCE

Sample COVariance

$$ \boxed{S_{xy} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_{i}-\bar{x})\times (y_{i}-\bar{y})}{n-1} }$$

Population COVariance

$$ \boxed{\sigma_{xy} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{N} (x_{i}-\mu_{x})\times (y_{i}-\mu_{y})}{N} }$$

this is basically calculating the relation between two features, as its sample data we will use sample format of the data

Covariance is giving the sense of the direction in which relation is moving

  1. > 0, the two variables move together
  2. < 0, the two variables move in opposite direction
  3. = 0, the two variables are independent

CORRELATION COVARIANCE

Instead of having big number can we bring it to smaller number to make it more intutive.

$$ \boxed{\frac{Cov(x,y)}{stdev(x) \times stdev(y)}}$$ $$ \boxed{-1 \leq correlation\_coifficent \leq 1}$$
  1. 1 means a perfect correlation between two variables
  2. 0 means no correlation
  3. -1 means negative correlation, meaning as one increases other variable decreases

SAMPLE MEAN

formula for Sample Mean :

$$ \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_{i}}{n} = \frac{(x_{1}+x_{2}+x_{3}+....+x_{n})}{n} $$

n = is the size of sample size

POPULATION MEAN

Formula for Population Mean :

$$ \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{N} x_{i}}{N} = \frac{(x_{1}+x_{2}+x_{3}+....+x_{N})}{N} $$

N = is the population

Both are some thing same. But used for calculation when we have sample vs popultation. Sample is random selection of data set, where as population is complete dataset.

VARIANCE

Variance shows the dispersion of the data around the mean value.

Sample Variance

$$ \boxed{S^{2} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_{i}-\bar{x})^{2}}{n} }$$

Population Variance

$$ \boxed{\sigma^{2} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{N} (x_{i}-\mu)^{2}}{N} }$$

STANDARD DEVIATION

Standard deviation is simple square root of the Variance


Sample Standard deviation

$$\boxed{S = \sqrt{S}}$$

Population Standard Deviation

$$\boxed{\sigma = \sqrt{\sigma}}$$

But the square is reducing the impact of the square root, but may not completely nullify it.

COEFFICIENT OF VARIATION

This is just a standard deviation divided by mean. This is also called as relative standard Deviation. This is simply telling the standard deviation wrt to mean.

Sample coifficent of vairance

$$\boxed{\hat{c}_{v} = \frac{S}{\bar{x}}}$$

Population coifficent of vairance

$$\boxed{c_{v} = \frac{\sigma}{\mu}}$$

When standard deviation is showing the variability for the dataset, why we need Coifficent of Variance. SD comparison for two different datasets does not have any meaning but comparing the Coifficent of Variance will make sense this is more of normalization of data t

ESTIMATORS

Maximum-likelihood estimation

In statistics Maximum likelihood estimation (MLE) is a method of estimating the parameters of a statistical model. The method obtains the parameter estimates by finding the parameter values that maximize the liklihood function.

Quasi-newton method

Quasi-Newton methods are the methods used to find either zeros or local maxima and minima of functions, as an alternative to Newton's method. They can be used if the 'Jacobian' or 'Hessian' is unavailable or too expensive to compute at every iteration.

Gradient descent algorithm

Its a first order iterative optimization algorithm for finding the minimum of a function. To find the local minimum of function using the gradient descent one takes steps proportional to the negative of the gradient of the function at the current point.
